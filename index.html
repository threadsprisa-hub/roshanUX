FAQ

YOUR
QUESTIONS.
MY
ANSWERS.

What kind of design work do you do?

I specialise in UI/UX design with a strong bias toward the UX side. User flows, wireframes, high-fidelity interfaces, and product concepts. Solving real problems — not decorating screens.

What tools do you use?

Primarily Figma for design and prototyping. Google Stitch for AI-assisted UI generation — which I refine through my own UX research and judgment. The tool serves the thinking, not the other way around.

Are you open to collaboration?

Currently sharpening skills through self-initiated work. Open to feedback-driven collaborations, peer reviews, and internship opportunities where I can contribute real value while learning.

How do you approach a new project?

Problem first — not pixels. Research, identify gaps, study users, structure the flow. The UI is always the last thing I design.

Why minimal?

We live in an age of short attention spans and information overload. When a design removes everything unnecessary, what remains carries more weight. Minimalism isn't absence — it's precision. Less noise. Clearer thinking. Better experience.
